# 20days — Brand brief (for designer)

## In one line
**20days** is a calm, private daily check-in app that helps people notice when life is quietly trending in the wrong direction — and decide what to do about it.

## What it does
You pick the **3 things that matter most** in your life (your "pillars" — e.g. Health, Relationships, Work). You score each one, once a day, in under a minute. The app quietly tracks the trend. When your scores show a **sustained downward drift**, it surfaces a gentle "checkpoint" moment that shows you your own data and asks: is this a real problem, or just a rough patch? From there you can keep going, re-think your priorities, or reach out for support.

## What it is NOT
Not a therapy app, not a diagnosis tool, not a habit-streak/gamification app. It doesn't try to *fix* you — it helps you *recognise* a pattern early, then points you to people who help (e.g. BetterHelp) if you want that. No badges, no streaks, no pressure, no guilt.

## Who it's for
Thoughtful, self-aware adults who want a low-effort way to keep an honest eye on their wellbeing — people who'd rather catch a slow decline early than realise it months too late. Think the quiet-confidence, data-as-self-knowledge audience (Whoop, Oura) — but for life satisfaction, not sleep.

## Personality / tone
- **Calm, honest, grown-up.** A trusted, understated companion — not a cheerleader, not a clinician.
- **Private and safe.** Everything stays on the user's device; that intimacy should be *felt* in the brand.
- **Reflective, not alarming.** Even "bad news" is delivered gently. Never a red-alert, blame, or crisis vibe.
- **Quietly premium.** Simple, spacious, considered. Fewer elements, more room to breathe.

## Visual direction (starting points, open to your take)
- **Logo:** a **simple text wordmark** — nothing fancy, no icon required. Set in **Bricolage Grotesque** (already chosen for headlines). Lowercase "20days" likely reads friendlier/calmer than caps; explore both. The "20" can carry a subtle idea (a period of observation before a verdict) but keep it clean.
- **Type system:** Bricolage Grotesque for the logo + headlines; **Roboto** for body/UI.
- **Color:** the product uses **Material 3 "Expressive"** and the palette **subtly shifts with the user's trend** (steadier/warmer when things are good, calmer/muted when they're not) — declining is a **warm muted tone, never an alarming red**. The brand needs a neutral, calm base identity that this dynamic system can live inside without clashing.
- **Feel:** soft, rounded, organic shapes; generous spacing; gentle motion.

## The name
"20days" nods to the rhythm: roughly 20 days of quiet data before a trend becomes meaningful — a period of honest observation, then a moment of reflection.

## Platform
iOS-first, indie/solo app. Small, focused, personal — the brand should feel handcrafted, not corporate.
