Full Stop

A visual language for the damo.land family of apps, starting with 20days. Typography-first, black and white, one blue.

The whole system hangs on one mark: the dot. It is the full stop that ends every headline, the unit that draws every chart (one day, one dot), and the indicator that marks now. One shape, three jobs — that repetition is what makes sister apps feel like siblings, the way every Brutally Human property leads with a lowercase dot-prefixed label. The interface speaks in short written sentences; the dot ends them.

01 · Principles, in order of precedence

Five rules

1

Say it, don't show it.

Typography is the interface. Every screen opens with a written sentence that already contains the answer — the headline is the data summary. Charts support the sentence, never replace it.

2

One day, one dot.

The dot is the only data primitive. A day is a dot, a score is a stack of dots, a window is a grid. If a visualisation can't be built from dots, redesign the visualisation.

3

Ink on paper, either way round.

Two surfaces only — Ink and Paper — swapped wholesale for dark mode. Chrome is never coloured and never grey-on-grey; grey exists only as faded ink for the past and the empty.

4

Colour is emphasis, not decoration.

Prime Blue means now and actionable: today's dot, links, the full stop. Four splash hues carry feeling — blurred, behind a word, one per screen at most. Colour never fills chrome, buttons or backgrounds.

5

Play lives in the punctuation.

Personality comes from motion and oversized punctuation — dots that pop in, the full stop that travels, a splash that breathes. Never from mascots, gradients-as-chrome, or extra colour.

02 · Colour

Two inks, one blue, four feelings

Warm bone, not clinical white; soft black, not #000. Dark mode is the same pair inverted — no third palette to maintain. Grey is always faded ink, derived from the foreground.

Prime Blue

The one true colour — and one hex, both modes. It sits a touch quieter on Ink; accepted, brand consistency wins. Means now (today's dot), actionable (links, the active tab), and brand (the full stop).

Prime Blue#2337FF · light and dark, one hex

Splash hues

Feeling-colours, and exceptional: most screens carry none. They appear two ways, both checkpoint-grade:

1 · Tinted dots (the default). When a state demands colour, the hue goes into the data — the dot cluster itself tints (whole cluster, one hue family; deeper tones on Paper, brighter on Ink). No glow behind dot clusters or sections, ever — the earlier cloud-behind-the-chart reads mushy and washes the labels.

2 · The glow (rarer). A blurred breath of colour behind a single compact block — a lone stat, a quoted note. Whatever sits on it takes its hue, tone on tone. Never behind words in headlines or running text; never behind dots. Warm and unhurried — no alarm red anywhere in the system.

03 · Typography

Funnel carries everything

Headlines, numbers and buttons in Funnel Display. Body and labels in Funnel Sans — its companion family, same skeleton, built to pair, free on Google Fonts with an Expo package. Recommended over Helvetica: Helvetica Neue is bundled on iOS but can't ship on Android or the web without licensing, and a grotesque sits flat next to Funnel's rounded geometry. (This page is set entirely in the pair.)

Six sizes, fixed tokens, nothing in between. Sizes are pt (RN dp); every text style in the app maps to exactly one row below. Body never drops under 16; Micro exists only for eyebrows and axis labels. Numbers always tabular.

04 · Signature elements

The kit of parts

steady

The full stop — square, locked

Every headline statement ends in a Prime Blue square stop — headlines ONLY, never buttons or body copy; questions keep their ?. A designed period, landing with a sprung quarter-turn. Standing rule: square = punctuation · circle = a day, a moment, now. Never swapped.

2.8 → 4.1 find support →

The arrow

The second punctuation mark. The stop ends a statement; the arrow points at movement — a score's travel written typographically (2.8 → 4.1), a forward action (find support →), ← back. Externals use → too (↗ is banned: Funnel has no glyph for it — it renders as a tofu box in-app). Set in Funnel: ink when it's data, blue when it acts. Never an icon. On hover the gap opens — the arrow springs ~5px forward (try the link above); in-app, the same nudge on press.

Overview

The rule

A full-width hairline in ink under every screen title. The one structural line — cards and shadows don't exist; sections are separated by rules, like a printed page.

The day-dot strip

The chart. Literally one day, one dot — each day's dot sits at its score's height, so the trend reads as a rising or falling line. Missed day = one hollow dot at the baseline. Today = blue. No bars, no lines, no axes.

Editorial actions

Secondary actions are underlined lowercase links, like a good essay. One solid ink pill per screen at most — plain label; the stop is headline punctuation, never button decoration. Screen-level secondary destinations collapse into a ··· menu (an ellipsis is punctuation, not a burger).

The travelling dot

One dot moves through the UI: it marks the active tab, fills the score picker, caps today's column, lands as the full stop after save. Motion identity = the dot arriving.

App icon & series mark

Ink square, short lowercase name, blue stop. Every future app: same recipe, different word. Instant family resemblance.

→ + ×

No icon set, no illustrations

Words do the work. The only glyphs are typographic — → + × — set in Funnel, so they read as type, not icons. If a pictogram is ever unavoidable, it's drawn on the dot grid (3×3 cells), same language as the days. "Illustration" is that idea at hero scale: constellations of faint dots for empty states. Never drawings, never an icon library.

05 · Voice

Calm sentences

Second person, present tense, sentences end with a full stop — never an exclamation mark. Links are lowercase. The app states what it sees ("Craft has been sliding for six days.") and asks instead of advising ("Does that match how it feels?"). Existing interpreter-not-author guardrails unchanged.

06 · Motion kit

Six verbs

Every animation in the system is one of six named verbs, all built from the dot and the rule — nothing else moves. Sprung, quick, quiet. Reduce Motion collapses every verb to a 150ms fade.

Pop

Dots spring in, staggered ~20ms. Screen entrance for any dot cluster; today's dot lands last.

Travel

One dot slides between states — tab to tab, score picker to save button. The dot never vanishes; it goes somewhere.

Settle

The square stop lands with a sprung quarter-turn. Ends every headline entrance, confirms every save. The signature move.

Hover me

Blink

The stop blinks twice on attention — hover on the web, scroll-into-focus in the app. A small acknowledgement, never a nag.

"skipped the gym again, no energy"

Breathe

The glow pulses over ~5s behind its lone stat or quote — never behind dot clusters. Ambient; at most one per screen.

Draw

The head-rule draws left to right on screen enter, then the stop settles. The opening line of every screen.

Web-only expressions

Two are live on this page (desktop). A Prime Blue square trails the system cursor — the arrow itself never changes or hides; the square is a companion. It spins as you move, and it morphs into a larger, fainter circle only over things that would show cursor: pointer — links, buttons, tabs. The morph is the pointer indicator; square = statement, circle = action. Separately, hovering a headline makes its stop settle, and arrow links open the gap — the arrow springs ~5px forward. The app has no cursor, so on iOS the equivalents remain press-squish, haptics, and Blink on scroll-into-view. Recommendation: cursor square on the future damo.land site — yes, at this intensity; in-app — no.

08 · Guardrails

Always / never

Always
  • Headline as a sentence, ending in the blue stop, rule beneath.
  • Data drawn in dots; today marked in Prime Blue.
  • Dark mode = Ink/Paper swap, nothing else changes.
  • State colour only at checkpoint-grade moments — tint the dots themselves, or one glow behind a lone stat/quote. Most screens: pure ink.
  • Movement written typographically — 2.8 → 4.1 — before any new chart form is invented.
  • Underlined lowercase links for secondary actions.
Never
  • Splash hues on words — or on chrome, buttons, tabs, backgrounds. Glows belong to data blocks only.
  • Ink-black or blue elements sitting on a glow — everything on a glow takes its hue.
  • Glows behind dot clusters, charts or whole sections — tint the dots instead.
  • Cards, drop shadows, or filled containers — rules divide, space groups.
  • Alarm red, exclamation marks, streak or guilt language.
  • A second display typeface, or colour-coded scores in the matrix.
  • Icons where a word fits. (Icons only where space truly forbids text.)
